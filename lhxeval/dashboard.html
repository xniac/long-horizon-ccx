Green = ON is better; red = ON is worse. pp = percentage points.

Paired statistics

$stat_rows

CIs are paired bootstrap (resampling (task,seed) pairs). McNemar is the exact paired test on per-trial pass/fail; "helped" = ON passed where OFF failed, "hurt" = the reverse.

pass@k vs pass^k curves (macro-averaged over tasks)

$curve_rows
kpass@k ONpass@k OFFpass^k ONpass^k OFF

pass@k (at least one of k succeeds) rises with k and is "exponentially forgiving"; pass^k (all k succeed) falls with k and is the honest reliability number. The ON/OFF gap on pass^k is the long-horizon payoff.

How to read this

The module can win two ways. (1) On the long-horizon-specific metrics (compaction-survival, resume-after-interruption, goal-drift, doom-loops), visible as higher reliability (pass^k) more than raw pass@1 — this is the signature in the simulated calibration. (2) On task completion itself, when a genuinely multi-session task has a subjective done-signal (the real v05/v06 A/Bs): here the gain shows as a large pass@1 gap while the long-horizon rows read “—” because those events never trigger. Neither pattern is a null result.
